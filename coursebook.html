Chapter 1: Workshop Safety, Ethics, and Orientation

Day 1 – Session 1: Occupational Health and Safety (OHS)

Duration: 40 Minutes

Type: Theory

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, students will be able to:

  • Understand the purpose and importance of Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) in an automotive workshop.
  • Identify common hazards found in automotive environments.
  • Recognize the technician’s legal and ethical responsibility to maintain safety.
  • Explain basic accident prevention practices.

1.1 Introduction to Safety in the Automotive Workshop

Time Allocation: 0–10 Minutes

Modern automotive workshops present a range of occupational hazards, including:

  • Sharp hand tools and cutting equipment
  • Rotating machinery and lifting devices
  • Exposure to flammable fuels, lubricants, and solvents
  • Noise, heat, and electrical shock risks

The purpose of OHS is to prevent injuries, protect life, and promote responsible conduct. Emphasis should be placed on the legal framework and the shared responsibility of employers, technicians, and supervisors to maintain a safe working environment.

1.2 Common Workshop Hazards

Time Allocation: 10–20 Minutes

Use visual aids such as posters and hazard charts to explain:

Hazard Type Examples
Physical Hazards Slippery floors, sharp tools, moving parts
Chemical Hazards Fumes from solvents, brake cleaner, fuels
Fire Hazards Fuel leaks, oil spills, exposed flames
Electrical Hazards Damaged wires, open terminals
Noise Hazards Power tools, compressors, grinders

Clarify the difference between a hazard (a potential source of harm) and a risk (the chance harm occurs).

1.3 Roles and Responsibilities in Workshop Safety

Time Allocation: 20–30 Minutes

Employer Responsibilities:

  • Provide a safe workspace.
  • Ensure training and equipment maintenance.
  • Supply proper tools and PPE.

Technician Responsibilities:

  • Always follow safety protocols.
  • Use PPE correctly.
  • Report unsafe conditions or injuries immediately.

Supervisor Responsibilities:

  • Enforce safety standards.
  • Conduct regular inspections and drills.
  • Maintain discipline on safety practices.

1.4 Safety Culture and Behavior

Time Allocation: 30–35 Minutes

Instructors should stress:

  • Safety is a mindset.
  • Clean-as-you-go and don’t bypass safety devices.
  • Positive safety behavior builds trust and professionalism.

1.5 Recap and Discussion

Time Allocation: 35–40 Minutes

Review Questions:

  1. What are three types of workshop hazards?
  2. What is the difference between a hazard and a risk?
  3. Who is responsible for enforcing safety?
