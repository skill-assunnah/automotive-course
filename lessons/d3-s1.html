Day 1 – Session 1: Introduction to Automotive Systems

⏱️ Full Class Duration: 40 Minutes

🔹 0–5 min: Brief Introduction to Vehicle Complexity & Importance of System Knowledge

  • Modern vehicles are complex machines made up of interdependent systems.
  • Each system—engine, transmission, electrical, etc.—must work together for reliable operation.
  • Technicians must understand how each system functions to diagnose and repair effectively.

💬 Example Prompt: “Have you ever wondered why a car doesn’t move even though the engine starts?”

This question helps students think about system interdependence.

  • Understanding systems = accurate diagnosis
  • Ensures safe maintenance
  • Builds confidence and professionalism

🔹 5–15 min: Lecture with Visuals/Board

🛠️ 1. Engine System

  • Purpose: Converts fuel energy into motion.
  • Combustion Basics: Fuel-air mix ignited to move pistons.
  • Main Parts:
    • Piston – Moves inside cylinder
    • Crankshaft – Converts motion to rotation
    • Camshaft – Controls valve timing
    • Valves – Intake/exhaust flow

⚙️ 2. Transmission System

  • Purpose: Transfers engine power to wheels.
  • Types:
    • Manual (MT) – Requires clutch
    • Automatic (AT) – Shifts by itself
    • CVT – Stepless, smooth transition

🔋 3. Electrical System

  • Purpose: Starts engine, powers lights, sensors, controls.
  • Components:
    • Battery – Stores and provides power
    • Starter Motor – Cranks the engine
    • Alternator – Recharges battery
    • Lights & Sensors – Important for visibility and feedback
  • ⚠️ Emphasize: Faulty battery/alternator = electrical issues or failure to start

🔹 15–25 min: Group Discussion & Comparison

  • Form groups of 3–5 students
  • Each group compares two systems (e.g., engine vs. electrical)
  • Discuss: Purpose, main components, dependency on other systems
  • Each group presents their key takeaways

🔹 25–35 min: Visual Demonstration (Images/Physical Parts)

  • Show real or model parts of:
    • Piston & crankshaft
    • Manual transmission gear
    • Battery & alternator
  • Ask: “What happens if this part fails?”
  • Use diagrams or animations if physical models are not available

🔹 35–40 min: Recap & Quick Quiz

  • Ask 3–5 oral questions:
    • What is the function of the crankshaft?
    • Difference between MT and AT?
    • Why is the alternator important?
  • Wrap up with key messages:
    • Each system has a role
    • System interdependence matters
    • As technicians, we must understand the basics thoroughly
