Professional Automotive Technician Training

3-Month Advanced Program

Organized by: As Sunnah Skill Development Institute

The Professional Automotive Technician Training is a rigorous, 12-week intensive program meticulously designed to develop skilled, job-ready automotive technicians. Conducted by As Sunnah Skill Development Institute, this program combines foundational mechanical knowledge with modern diagnostic expertise, ensuring participants are fully prepared for careers in the local and international automotive service industry.

Rooted in hands-on learning and real-world problem-solving, the curriculum balances technical skill-building, diagnostic confidence, and professional work ethics, producing technicians capable of servicing both conventional and modern vehicles. While secular in nature, the course subtly integrates Islamic ethical values—like responsibility, safety, and integrity—into areas such as workplace conduct, customer service, and safety awareness.

  • Duration: 3 Months (12 Weeks, Saturday–Thursday, 40 hours/week, ~240 hours total)
  • Course Format:
    • 50% Theory (lectures, diagrams, discussions)
    • 40% Practical (workshops, system servicing, diagnostics)
    • 10% Assessment (tests, evaluations, capstone)
  • Target Group:
    • Youth and adults (minimum SSC or equivalent)
    • Aspiring mechanics, entrepreneurs, or job seekers in the automotive field
  • Certification:
    • Upon completion, participants will earn the Professional Automotive Technician Certificate (3-Month) issued by As Sunnah Skill Development Institute
  • Training Infrastructure:
    • Classrooms with multimedia setup (bilingual Bangla/English materials)
    • Hands-on workshops with complete toolkits (including torque wrenches, OBD-II scanners, fuel system testers)
    • Access to petrol & diesel engine models for servicing practice
    • Qualified trainers with industry and instructional experience

Basic Module (Weeks 1–4)

  • OHS: Workplace safety regulations, accident prevention, safe material handling, emergency procedures, hazard identification.
  • PPE: Types (gloves, goggles, helmets, etc.), selection, usage, inspection, replacement.
  • Workshop Ethics: Professional conduct, punctuality, cleanliness, teamwork, tool responsibility.
  • Identify and Inspect: Recognize hand tools (spanners, pliers) and power tools (drills, grinders); ensure suitability.
  • Proper Use: Correct techniques, handling, torque application, manufacturer’s instructions.
  • Maintenance: Clean tools, lubricate, store properly, routine checks.
  • Engine: Types (Inline, V-type), 4-stroke cycle, key components, combustion process.
  • Fuel System: Tank, pump, filter, injectors; servicing, pressure checks, filter replacement.
  • Cooling: Radiator, thermostat, hoses; heat exchange, leak checks, coolant maintenance.
  • Transmission: Manual/automatic, clutch, CVT; lubrication, adjustments.
  • Brake: Disc/drum, ABS; fluid bleeding, pad replacement.
  • Steering/Suspension: Steering types, suspension components; inspections.
  • Electrical: Battery, alternator, fuses; diagnostics, wiring, fuse replacement.
  • Exhaust: Manifold, catalytic converter; leakage, emissions checks.
  • SI vs CI Engines: Spark Ignition vs Compression Ignition differences.
  • Operating Cycles: 4-stroke (Otto, Diesel), 2-stroke basics.
  • Key Components: Cylinder block, pistons, crankshaft, valves, lubrication.
  • Function: Energy conversion to mechanical power.
  • Lubrication/Cooling: Oil flow, heat management, measurements (bore, stroke).
  • Written Test: MCQ/short questions on safety, tools, systems.
  • Practical: Tool identification, engine components, servicing checks.
  • Oral/Viva: Safety procedures, system functions.
  • Tool Handling: Observation of correct usage, safety compliance.
  • Main Components of Fuel System: Fuel tank, fuel pump (mechanical/electrical), fuel filter, fuel lines, fuel injectors, carburetor (in older models), fuel rail.
  • Function of Fuel System: Stores and supplies clean fuel to the engine at the correct pressure and flow rate for combustion; enables efficient engine performance.
  • Servicing and Replacing Tasks: Inspect and replace fuel filters, clean injectors, test fuel pressure, check for leaks in hoses/lines, replace worn-out pumps or others.
  • Main Components of Cooling System: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, radiator fan (mechanical/electric), coolant reservoir, hoses, temperature sensor.
  • Function of Cooling System: Regulates engine temperature by dissipating heat from the engine; prevents overheating, ensures optimal engine efficiency.
  • Servicing Tasks: Check coolant level and condition, pressure-test the radiator cap, flush and refill coolant, inspect/replace hoses and clamps, test thermostat and fan operation.
  • Main Components of Lubricating System: Oil sump (pan), oil pump, oil filter, pressure relief valve, oil galleries, dipstick.
  • Function of Lubricating System: Reduces friction between engine parts, removes heat, cleans internal engine components, provides sealing and rust protection.
  • Servicing Tasks: Drain old engine oil, replace oil filter, refill with correct grade oil, check oil pressure, inspect for leaks and worn gaskets.
  • Written Test: Questions on fuel, cooling, and lubrication systems — covering components, functions, and servicing steps.
  • Practical Evaluation: Demonstration of draining and refilling coolant and engine oil, replacing fuel/oil filters, leak inspection.
  • Tool Handling Observation: Evaluate proper use of tools such as oil drain pan, filter wrench, funnel, and coolant tester.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Explain purpose of thermostat, oil pressure system, and fuel delivery system in own words.
  • Main Components of Starting System: Battery, ignition switch, starter motor, solenoid, flywheel ring gear, wiring harness.
  • Function of Starting System: Converts electrical energy from the battery into mechanical motion to crank and start the engine.
  • Servicing Tasks: Check battery charge and connections, test starter motor operation, inspect solenoid relay, clean terminals, diagnose abnormal noises.
  • Main Components of Ignition System: Ignition coil, distributor (in older systems), spark plugs, ignition switch, crankshaft position sensor, control module (in modern vehicles), wiring harness.
  • Function of Ignition System: Provides high-voltage spark at the right time to ignite the air-fuel mixture in petrol (SI) engines.
  • Servicing and Replacing Tasks: Inspect and replace spark plugs, test and replace ignition coil, adjust timing (if applicable), check wiring and connectors, diagnose misfire or no-start conditions.
  • Main Components of Air Induction System: Air filter, intake manifold, throttle body, air flow sensor (MAF/MAP), intercooler (in turbo engines), ducting and hoses.
  • Function of Air Induction System: Supplies clean, filtered air to the engine; regulates air quantity for combustion; contributes to fuel efficiency and emissions control.
  • Repair and Servicing Tasks: Clean or replace air filter, inspect ducting for cracks or leaks, clean throttle body, test air sensors, reseal or replace gaskets if needed.
  • Written Test: Multiple choice or short answers covering ignition, starting, and air induction system components, functions, and procedures.
  • Practical Evaluation: Demonstrate spark plug replacement, starter motor testing, air filter servicing, throttle body cleaning.
  • Tool Handling Observation: Safe and correct use of multimeter, plug spanner, throttle cleaner, and basic diagnostic tools.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Explain ignition sequence, signs of faulty air intake, or differences between SI and CI ignition methods.
  • Main Components of Exhaust System: Exhaust manifold, catalytic converter, muffler/silencer, exhaust pipes, oxygen sensors, resonator.
  • Function of Exhaust System: Directs exhaust gases safely away from the engine and cabin, reduces emissions, and minimizes noise.
  • Repair and Servicing Tasks: Inspect and replace damaged pipes, clean or replace catalytic converter, check oxygen sensors, repair leaks, and ensure mounting.
  • 4-Stroke SI Engine Cycle: Intake, Compression, Power, and Exhaust strokes; how each stage works to convert fuel into mechanical energy.
  • Function of SI Engine: Uses spark plug ignition to ignite the air-fuel mixture in petrol engines for power generation.
  • SI Engine Tune-Up Tasks: Adjust ignition timing, clean or replace spark plugs, check and adjust carburetor/fuel injection, inspect valves and ignition system components.
  • 4-Stroke Diesel Engine Cycle: Intake, Compression, Power, and Exhaust strokes; fuel injected under high pressure, ignited by compressed air heat.
  • Function of Diesel Engine: Converts fuel into mechanical power using compression ignition; typically, more torque and fuel-efficient than SI engines.
  • SI Engine Tune-Up Tasks: Adjust injector timing, clean/replace fuel injectors, check glow plugs, inspect valves and filters, verify compression levels.
  • Written Test: Questions covering SI and Diesel engine cycles, functions, and tune-up procedures.
  • Practical Evaluation: Demonstrate spark plug replacement, injector cleaning, timing adjustments, and valve inspection.
  • Tool Handling Observation: Correct use of timing light, injector cleaner, compression tester, and hand tools.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Explain differences between SI and Diesel engines, tune-up importance, and diagnostic steps.

Intermediate Module (Weeks 5–8)

  • Main Components of Power Train System: Clutch assembly, transmission (manual/automatic), driveshaft, differential, axle shafts, CV joints.
  • Function of Power Train System: Transmits engine power to wheels, enabling vehicle movement with appropriate torque and speed control.
  • Servicing Tasks: Inspect clutch wear, adjust linkage, change transmission fluid, check driveshaft and CV joints, replace worn components.
  • Main Components of Steering System: Steering wheel, steering column, steering rack and pinion, tie rods, power steering pump, hydraulic lines.
  • Function of Steering System: Allows the driver to control the direction of the vehicle by turning the front wheels.
  • Servicing Tasks: Inspect and adjust steering linkage, check power steering fluid level and condition, repair leaks, replace worn tie rods or seals.
  • Written Test: Questions covering powertrain and steering system components, functions, and servicing tasks.
  • Practical Evaluation: Demonstrate inspection and adjustment of steering linkage, clutch adjustment, and transmission fluid check.
  • Tool Handling Observation: Proper use of torque wrench, fluid tester, steering wheel puller, and inspection tools.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Explain powertrain functions, steering system components, and common troubleshooting methods.
  • Main Components of Suspension System: Springs (coil, leaf), shock absorbers, control arms, bushings, struts, stabilizer bars.
  • Function of Suspension System: Absorbs shocks from road irregularities, maintains vehicle stability and ride comfort.
  • Servicing Tasks: Inspect and replace worn shocks and bushings, lubricate joints, check alignment, test suspension travel and rebound.
  • Function of Wheel Balancing and Alignment: Ensures even tire wear, smooth vehicle handling, reduces vibration, and improves fuel efficiency.
  • Servicing Tasks: Use balancing machines to correct wheel imbalance, adjust camber, toe, and caster angles; inspect tires and suspension for alignment.
  • Written Test: Questions on suspension, wheel balancing, and alignment theory and procedures.
  • Practical Evaluation: Demonstrate wheel balancing, alignment adjustments, and suspension inspection.
  • Tool Handling Observation: Proper use of balancing machine, alignment tools, and suspension measuring instruments.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Explain importance of wheel alignment and balancing and common symptoms of misalignment.
  • Types of Brake System: Disc brakes, drum brakes, anti-lock braking system (ABS), hydraulic and pneumatic systems.
  • Main Components of Brake System: Brake pedal, master cylinder, brake lines, brake calipers, brake pads/shoes, wheel cylinders.
  • Function of Brake System: Converts driver input into hydraulic pressure to slow or stop vehicle by applying friction to wheels.
  • Repair and Servicing Tasks: Inspect and replace brake pads/shoes, bleed brake lines, check and refill brake fluid, repair leaks, inspect ABS sensors and wiring.
  • Main Components of A/C System: Compressor, condenser, evaporator, expansion valve, refrigerant lines, blower motor.
  • Function of the A/C System: Cools and dehumidifies air inside the vehicle cabin by circulating refrigerant.
  • Basic A/C Maintenance Tasks: Check refrigerant level and refill, inspect belts and hoses, clean condenser and evaporator coils, replace cabin air filter, check blower.
  • Written Test: Questions on components, functions, and A/C system maintenance.
  • Practical Evaluation: Demonstrate brake pad replacement, brake bleeding, A/C refrigerant level check, and filter replacement.
  • Tool Handling Observation: Proper use of brake bleeding kit, refrigerant gauge, and A/C diagnostic tools.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Explain brake system safety checks and common A/C system issues.
  • Main Components of Electrical/Electronics:
    • Charging System: Alternator, battery, voltage regulator, wiring; responsible for electrical energy generation and storage.
    • Lighting System: Headlights, tail lights, indicators, brake lights, interior lights; ensures vehicle visibility and signaling.
    • Horn and Alarm System: Audio signaling and security alarms for safety and theft prevention.
    • Interior Electronics: Power windows, door locks, infotainment systems, climate controls.
    • Wiring and Fuses: Wiring harnesses, fuse boxes, connectors; essential for circuit protection and connectivity.
    • Instrument Cluster: Speedometer, tachometer, fuel gauge, warning lights; provides driver information.
    • Sensor, Actuator, ECU: Input devices (temp, oxygen sensors), output devices (fuel injectors, relays), and electronic control unit for engine and system management.
  • Repair/Servicing Tasks: Diagnose electrical faults using scan tools, replace faulty wiring/fuses, repair or replace sensors and actuators, troubleshoot ECU errors.
  • Main Components of EFI System: Fuel injectors, electronic control unit (ECU), fuel pump, sensors (MAF, oxygen, throttle position), fuel rail, wiring harness.
  • Function of the EFI System: Controls precise fuel delivery electronically for optimal combustion, improved efficiency, and reduced emissions.
  • Servicing/Repair Tasks: Diagnose EFI faults using scan tools, clean or replace fuel injectors, test and replace sensors, inspect wiring and connectors, calibrate ECU.
  • Main Components of Charging System: Alternator, voltage regulator, battery, drive belt, wiring harness.
  • Function of Charging System: Generates and regulates electrical power to charge the battery and power vehicle electrical systems.
  • Servicing Tasks: Test alternator output, inspect and replace drive belts, check voltage regulator, clean battery terminals, check wiring for corrosion.
  • Written Test: Covering EFI system, charging system, and general vehicle electronics.
  • Practical Evaluation: Diagnose EFI faults using scan tools, test alternator and battery condition, replace faulty sensors or wiring components.
  • Tool Handling Observation: Use of diagnostic tools such as multimeter, scan tools, and battery testers.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Explain EFI functions, charging system components, and common troubleshooting procedures.

Advanced Module (Weeks 9–11)

  • Main Components of LPG and CNG Kits: Pressure regulator, fuel injectors or mixers, gas cylinders, electronic control unit (ECU), safety valves, hoses, and mounting brackets.
  • Installation Tasks: Safely mount gas cylinders, connect fuel lines and regulators, install ECU and sensors, perform leak tests, ensure compliance with safety standards, calibrate system for engine compatibility.
  • Purpose of Calibration: Ensure optimal air–fuel mixture for performance, fuel efficiency, and reduced emissions; prevent engine knocking or misfiring.
  • Steps to Test and Adjust Calibration: Connect diagnostic tool or manual tuning device, measure pressure and flow, fine-tune fuel delivery using ECU or regulator, perform test drive, monitor exhaust and engine response, adjust timing if required.
  • Written Test: Questions on components and functions of LPG/CNG kits, safety protocols, and calibration procedures.
  • Practical Evaluation: Demonstrate installation of gas cylinder and regulator, leak test, calibration using scanner or manual method.
  • Tool Handling Observation: Proper use of gas leak detectors, calibration tools, and ECU interfaces.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Explain the importance of correct calibration, safety measures, and fuel switching process.
  • Main Components to Be Serviced: CNG/LPG tank and valves, regulator, injectors or mixer, ECU, filters, fuel lines, pressure relief devices.
  • Typical Servicing Tasks: Inspect for gas leaks, test regulator pressure, clean or replace filters, check injector/mixer function, verify ECU diagnostics, perform routine safety inspection.
  • Written Test: On CNG/LPG system functions, servicing intervals, and safety checks.
  • Practical Evaluation: Pressure tests, leak detection, component cleaning, system reset.
  • Tool Handling Observation: Use of gas leak detectors, pressure gauges, and protective equipment.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Explain routine CNG/LPG maintenance, symptoms of failure, and emergency handling procedures.
  • Types of Vehicles: Mild hybrid, full hybrid, plug-in hybrid (PHEV), battery electric vehicles (BEV), extended-range EVs.
  • Key Components: Electric motor, inverter, battery pack, battery management system (BMS), regenerative braking, high-voltage cables.
  • Function of Hybrid/EV: Use electric motors alone or in combination with internal combustion engines to reduce emissions and improve fuel efficiency.
  • Benefits of Hybrid/EV: Lower operating costs, reduced emissions, improved energy efficiency, government incentives, quieter operation.
  • Written Test: Questions on hybrid/EV types, components (motor, inverter, battery), benefits, and basic functions.
  • Practical Evaluation: Identify hybrid/EV system components on a training vehicle or diagram, simulate safe shutdown of HV system.
  • Tool Handling Observation: Proper use of insulated tools, safety gloves, and voltage testers for EVs.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Regenerative braking, hybrid configurations, and high-voltage safety.
  • Key Components: Sensors (e.g., oxygen, throttle, crankshaft), actuators (e.g., injectors, relays), ECUs, wiring, CAN Bus system.
  • Tools Used: Multimeter, OBD-II scanner, oscilloscope, diagnostic software tools.
  • Diagnostic Steps: Retrieve fault codes using OBD-II, interpret live data, perform guided tests, inspect wiring/sensor signal, clear fault codes and validate repair.
  • Common ADAS Components: Cameras, ultrasonic sensors, radar, LiDAR, ECUs, steering and braking actuators.
  • Main Functions of ADAS: Lane-keeping assist, adaptive cruise control, automatic emergency braking (AEB), blind-spot detection, parking assist.
  • Benefits of ADAS: Enhances driver safety, reduces collisions, supports semi-autonomous driving, lowers driver fatigue.
  • Written Test: Questions on automotive electronics, OBD diagnostics, and ADAS components and functions.
  • Practical Evaluation: Retrieve and interpret OBD fault codes, perform sensor tests, demonstrate basic ADAS function check (e.g., camera/sensor alignment).
  • Tool Handling Observation: Safe use of diagnostic tools: OBD-II scanner, multimeter, calibration interface tools.
  • Oral/Viva Assessment: Explain CAN Bus system, ADAS safety features, and sensor/actuator roles in modern diagnostics.

Week 12: Final Examination & Certification

  • Final Written Examination: Comprehensive theory test covering all modules: safety, systems, diagnostics, EFI, hybrid/EV, and ADAS.
  • Final Practical Examination: Hands-on tasks from each module — diagnostics, servicing key systems (engine, brakes, electronics), EFI tuning, A/C servicing, etc.
  • Oral/Viva Evaluation: Panel Q&A testing conceptual understanding, troubleshooting approach, and communication skills.
  • Certification & Feedback: Issuing certificates to qualified trainees, performance review, career guidance, and final feedback session.
