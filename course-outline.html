Automotive Technology Course Outline

Basic Module (Weeks 1–4)

1.1 Safety and Hazard

1.1.1 OHS (Occupational Health and Safety): Workplace safety regulations, accident prevention, safe material handling, emergency procedures, hazard identification.

1.1.2 PPE (Personal Protective Equipment): Types (gloves, goggles, helmets, overalls, earplugs), selection, usage, inspection, replacement.

1.1.3 Workshop Ethics: Professional conduct, punctuality, discipline, cleanliness, teamwork, tool and environment responsibility.

1.2 Using Hand and Power Tools

1.2.1 Identify and Inspect Tools: Recognize hand tools (spanners, pliers, hammers) and power tools (drills, grinders); ensure suitability.

1.2.2 Proper Use of Tools: Correct techniques, handling positions, torque application, manufacturer’s instructions.

1.2.3 Cleaning and Maintenance: Clean tools, lubricate, store properly, routine maintenance checks.

1.3 Interpret Major Systems of Automobiles

1.3.1 Engine System: Engine types (Inline, V-type), 4-stroke cycle, cylinder block, timing system, crankshaft, oil sump, head gasket, combustion.

1.3.2 Fuel System & Servicing: Fuel tank, lines, filter, pump, carburetor/injector, throttle body; servicing, pressure check, filter replacement.

1.3.3 Cooling System & Servicing: Radiator, fan, thermostat, water pump, hoses; heat exchange, leak checks, radiator flushing, coolant maintenance.

1.3.4 Transmission System: Manual/automatic gearboxes, clutch, CVT, torque converter; lubrication, clutch adjustment, oil change.

1.3.5 Brake System: Disc/drum brakes, master/wheel cylinder, lines, caliper, ABS; fluid bleeding, pad replacement.

1.3.6 Steering and Suspension: Steering types (manual, power, EPS), suspension (coil/leaf spring, shock absorber); inspection.

1.3.7 Electrical System: Battery, alternator, starter, fuses, lighting; diagnostics, wiring trace, fuse replacement.

1.3.8 Exhaust System: Manifold, catalytic converter, muffler, tailpipe; leakage, rust, emissions, sound reduction.

1.4 Engine System

1.4.1 Types of Engines (SI vs CI): Spark Ignition vs Compression Ignition; fuel systems and diagnostics.

1.4.2 Operating Cycles: 4-stroke (Otto, Diesel), 2-stroke; troubleshooting foundation.

1.4.3 Key Components: Cylinder block, piston, crankshaft, camshaft, valves, timing chain/belt, spark plug (SI), injector (CI), lubrication.

1.4.4 Function: Converts energy to mechanical power.

1.4.5 Lubrication Path: Oil flow through moving parts, wear reduction.

1.4.6 Cooling Overview: Engine cooling methods.

1.4.7 Measurements: Bore, stroke, displacement, compression ratio.

1.5 Assessments

1.5.1 Written Test: MCQs/short questions on safety, tools, systems.

1.5.2 Practical Evaluation: Tool/component identification, basic servicing.

1.5.3 Oral/Viva: Safety, system functions, component roles.

1.5.4 Tool Handling: Correct usage, posture, safety compliance.

2.1 Servicing and Replacing Fuel System Components

2.1.1 Components: Fuel tank, pump, filter, lines, injectors, carburetor, fuel rail.

2.1.2 Function: Supplies clean fuel at correct pressure for combustion.

2.1.3 Servicing: Inspect/replace filters, clean injectors, test pressure, check leaks.

2.2 Servicing and Maintaining Cooling System

2.2.1 Components: Radiator, water pump, thermostat, fan, coolant reservoir, hoses, sensor.

2.2.2 Function: Regulates engine temperature, prevents overheating.

2.2.3 Servicing: Check coolant, pressure-test cap, flush/refill, inspect hoses, test thermostat/fan.

2.3 Servicing and Maintaining Lubrication System

2.3.1 Components: Oil sump, pump, filter, pressure valve, galleries, dipstick.

2.3.2 Function: Reduces friction, removes heat, cleans engine, protects against rust.

2.3.3 Servicing: Drain oil, replace filter, refill oil, check pressure, inspect leaks.

2.4 Assessments

2.4.1 Written Test: Fuel, cooling, lubrication systems components/functions/servicing.

2.4.2 Practical Evaluation: Drain/refill coolant/oil, replace filters, leak inspection.

2.4.3 Tool Handling: Oil drain pan, filter wrench, funnel, coolant tester.

2.4.4 Oral/Viva: Explain thermostat, oil pressure, fuel delivery purpose.

3.1 Diagnosing and Servicing Starting System

3.1.1 Components: Battery, ignition switch, starter motor, solenoid, flywheel ring gear, wiring.

3.1.2 Function: Converts electrical energy to crank engine.

3.1.3 Servicing: Check battery, test starter, inspect solenoid, clean terminals, diagnose noises.

3.2 Servicing and Replacing Ignition System

3.2.1 Components: Ignition coil, distributor, spark plugs, switch, crankshaft sensor, control module, wiring.

3.2.2 Function: Provides spark to ignite air-fuel mixture in SI engines.

3.2.3 Servicing: Inspect/replace spark plugs, test/replace coil, adjust timing, check wiring, diagnose misfire/no-start.

3.3 Repair and Service Air Induction System

3.3.1 Components: Air filter, intake manifold, throttle body, air flow sensor, intercooler, hoses.

3.3.2 Function: Supplies clean air, regulates combustion air, improves efficiency/emissions.

3.3.3 Servicing: Clean/replace filter, inspect ducts, clean throttle body, test sensors, reseal gaskets.

3.4 Assessments

3.4.1 Written Test: Ignition, starting, air induction components/functions/procedures.

3.4.2 Practical Evaluation: Spark plug replacement, starter testing, air filter/throttle body servicing.

3.4.3 Tool Handling: Multimeter, plug spanner, throttle cleaner, diagnostic tools.

3.4.4 Oral/Viva: Ignition sequence, faulty intake signs, SI vs CI ignition.

4.1 Servicing and Repairing Exhaust System

4.1.1 Components: Exhaust manifold, catalytic converter, muffler, pipes, oxygen sensors, resonator.

4.1.2 Function: Directs exhaust gases, reduces emissions/noise.

4.1.3 Servicing: Inspect/replace pipes, clean/replace converter, check sensors, repair leaks.

4.2 Perform Spark Ignition (SI) Engine Tune-Up

4.2.1 Cycle: Intake, Compression, Power, Exhaust strokes; converts fuel to energy.

4.2.2 Function: Uses spark plug to ignite air-fuel mixture in SI engines.

4.2.3 Tune-Up: Adjust timing, clean/replace spark plugs, check carburetor/injection, inspect valves/ignition.

4.3 Perform Compression Ignition (Diesel) Engine Tune-Up

4.3.1 Cycle: Intake, Compression, Power, Exhaust; fuel ignited by compressed air.

4.3.2 Function: Converts fuel to power via compression ignition; high torque, efficient.

4.3.3 Tune-Up: Adjust injector timing, clean/replace injectors, check glow plugs, verify compression.

4.4 Assessments

4.4.1 Written Test: SI/Diesel cycles, functions, tune-up procedures.

4.4.2 Practical Evaluation: Spark plug/injector replacement, timing adjustments, valve inspection.

4.4.3 Tool Handling: Timing light, injector cleaner, compression tester.

4.4.4 Oral/Viva: SI vs Diesel differences, tune-up importance, diagnostics.

Intermediate Module (Weeks 5–8)

5.1 Overhauling and Servicing Powertrain System

5.1.1 Components: Clutch, transmission, driveshaft, differential, axle shafts, CV joints.

5.1.2 Function: Transmits engine power to wheels, torque/speed control.

5.1.3 Servicing: Inspect clutch, adjust linkage, change fluid, check driveshaft/CV joints.

5.2 Servicing and Repairing Steering System

5.2.1 Components: Steering wheel, column, rack/pinion, tie rods, power steering pump.

5.2.2 Function: Controls vehicle direction via front wheels.

5.2.3 Servicing: Inspect/adjust linkage, check fluid, repair leaks, replace tie rods/seals.

5.3 Assessments

5.3.1 Written Test: Powertrain/steering components, functions, servicing.

5.3.2 Practical Evaluation: Steering linkage/clutch adjustment, fluid check.

5.3.3 Tool Handling: Torque wrench, fluid tester, steering puller.

5.3.4 Oral/Viva: Powertrain/steering functions, troubleshooting.

6.1 Servicing and Inspecting Suspension System

6.1.1 Components: Springs, shocks, control arms, bushings, struts, stabilizer bars.

6.1.2 Function: Absorbs shocks, maintains stability/ride comfort.

6.1.3 Servicing: Inspect/replace shocks/bushings, lubricate joints, check alignment.

6.2 Wheel Balancing and Alignment

6.2.1 Function: Ensures even tire wear, smooth handling, reduced vibration.

6.2.2 Servicing: Balance wheels, adjust camber/toe/caster, inspect tires/suspension.

6.3 Assessments

6.3.1 Written Test: Suspension, balancing, alignment theory/procedures.

6.3.2 Practical Evaluation: Wheel balancing, alignment, suspension inspection.

6.3.3 Tool Handling: Balancing machine, alignment tools, suspension instruments.

6.3.4 Oral/Viva: Importance of alignment/balancing, misalignment symptoms.

7.1 Servicing and Repairing Braking System

7.1.1 Types: Disc, drum, ABS, hydraulic/pneumatic systems.

7.1.2 Components: Brake pedal, master cylinder, lines, calipers, pads/shoes, wheel cylinders.

7.1.3 Function: Converts driver input to hydraulic pressure to slow/stop vehicle.

7.1.4 Servicing: Inspect/replace pads/shoes, bleed lines, check/refill fluid, repair leaks, inspect ABS.

7.2 Perform Basic Maintenance of Air Conditioning System

7.2.1 Components: Compressor, condenser, evaporator, expansion valve, refrigerant lines, blower.

7.2.2 Function: Cools/dehumidifies cabin air via refrigerant circulation.

7.2.3 Maintenance: Check/refill refrigerant, inspect belts/hoses, clean coils, replace cabin filter, check blower.

7.3 Assessments

7.3.1 Written Test: Brake/A/C components, functions, maintenance.

7.3.2 Practical Evaluation: Brake pad replacement, bleeding, refrigerant check, filter replacement.

7.3.3 Tool Handling: Brake bleeding kit, refrigerant gauge, A/C diagnostic tools.

7.3.4 Oral/Viva: Brake safety checks, A/C system issues.

8.1 Servicing Body Electrical and Electronics System

8.1.1 Components: Charging, lighting, horn, interior electronics, wiring, fuses, instrument cluster, sensors, actuators, ECU.

8.1.2 Charging System: Alternator, battery, voltage regulator; generates/stores electrical energy.

8.1.3 Lighting System: Headlights, tail lights, indicators; ensures visibility/signaling.

8.1.4 Horn/Alarm: Audio signaling, security alarms.

8.1.5 Interior Electronics: Power windows, locks, infotainment, climate controls.

8.1.6 Wiring/Fuses: Harnesses, fuse boxes, connectors; circuit protection.

8.1.7 Instrument Cluster: Speedometer, tachometer, gauges, warning lights.

8.1.8 Sensors, Actuators, ECU: Temp/oxygen sensors, injectors, relays; engine/system management.

8.1.9 Servicing: Diagnose faults, replace wiring/fuses, repair sensors/actuators, troubleshoot ECU.

8.2 Diagnosing, Servicing, and Repairing EFI System

8.2.1 Components: Fuel injectors, ECU, fuel pump, sensors (MAF, oxygen, throttle), fuel rail, wiring.

8.2.2 Function: Controls precise fuel delivery for combustion, efficiency, reduced emissions.

8.2.3 Servicing: Diagnose faults, clean/replace injectors, test sensors, inspect wiring, calibrate ECU.

8.3 Servicing and Repairing Charging System

8.3.1 Components: Alternator, voltage regulator, battery, drive belt, wiring.

8.3.2 Function: Generates/regulates power to charge battery, power systems.

8.3.3 Servicing: Test alternator, inspect/replace belts, check regulator, clean terminals, inspect wiring.

8.4 Assessments

8.4.1 Written Test: EFI, charging, vehicle electronics.

8.4.2 Practical Evaluation: Diagnose EFI, test alternator/battery, replace sensors/wiring.

8.4.3 Tool Handling: Multimeter, scan tools, battery testers.

8.4.4 Oral/Viva: EFI functions, charging components, troubleshooting.

Advanced Module (Weeks 9–11)

9.1 Installing LPG and CNG Conversion Kit

9.1.1 Components: Pressure regulator, injectors/mixers, gas cylinders, ECU, safety valves.

9.1.2 Installation: Mount cylinders, connect fuel lines, install ECU/sensors, leak test, calibrate.

9.2 Testing and Adjusting LPG/CNG Calibration

9.2.1 Purpose: Optimize air-fuel mixture, prevent knocking/misfiring.

9.2.2 Steps: Use diagnostic tool, measure pressure/flow, fine-tune ECU, test drive.

9.3 Assessments

9.3.1 Written Test: LPG/CNG components, safety, calibration.

9.3.2 Practical Evaluation: Install cylinder/regulator, leak test, calibration.

9.3.3 Tool Handling: Gas leak detectors, calibration tools, ECU interfaces.

9.3.4 Oral/Viva: Calibration importance, safety, fuel switching.

10.1 Servicing Automotive CNG and LPG System

10.1.1 Components: Tank/valves, regulator, injectors/mixer, ECU, filters.

10.1.2 Servicing: Inspect leaks, test regulator, clean/replace filters, ECU diagnostics.

10.2 Assessments

10.2.1 Written Test: CNG/LPG functions, servicing, safety.

10.2.2 Practical Evaluation: Pressure tests, leak detection, component cleaning.

10.2.3 Tool Handling: Gas leak detectors, pressure gauges, PPE.

10.2.4 Oral/Viva: Maintenance, failure symptoms, emergency procedures.

10.3 Basics of Hybrid and Electric Vehicle (EV)

10.3.1 Types: Mild/full/plug-in hybrid, BEV, extended-range EVs.

10.3.2 Components: Electric motor, inverter, battery, BMS, regenerative braking.

10.3.3 Function: Reduce emissions, improve efficiency with electric motors.

10.3.4 Benefits: Lower costs, reduced emissions, quieter operation.

10.4 Assessments

10.4.1 Written Test: Hybrid/EV types, components, benefits.

10.4.2 Practical Evaluation: Identify components, simulate HV shutdown.

10.4.3 Tool Handling: Insulated tools, safety gloves, voltage testers.

10.4.4 Oral/Viva: Regenerative braking, hybrid configs, HV safety.

11.1 Basics Automotive Electronics and Advanced Diagnostics

11.1.1 Components: Sensors (oxygen, throttle, crankshaft), actuators (injectors, relays), ECUs, wiring, CAN Bus.

11.1.2 Tools: Multimeter, OBD-II scanner, oscilloscope, diagnostic software.

11.1.3 Diagnostics: Retrieve fault codes, interpret live data, perform tests, inspect wiring, clear codes.

11.2 Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS)

11.2.1 Components: Cameras, ultrasonic sensors, radar, LiDAR, ECUs, steering/braking actuators.

11.2.2 Functions: Lane-keeping, adaptive cruise, emergency braking, blind-spot detection, parking assist.

11.2.3 Benefits: Enhances safety, reduces collisions, supports semi-autonomous driving.

11.3 Assessments

11.3.1 Written Test: Electronics, OBD diagnostics, ADAS components/functions.

11.3.2 Practical Evaluation: Retrieve/interpret OBD codes, sensor tests, ADAS function check.

11.3.3 Tool Handling: OBD-II scanner, multimeter, calibration tools.

11.3.4 Oral/Viva: CAN Bus, ADAS features, sensor/actuator roles.

Week 12: Final Examination & Certification

12.1 Final Assessments

12.1.1 Written Examination: Comprehensive test on all modules.

12.1.2 Practical Examination: Diagnostics, servicing, EFI tuning, A/C.

12.1.3 Oral/Viva: Conceptual understanding, troubleshooting, communication.

12.1.4 Certification: Issue certificates, performance review, career guidance.
