Glossary of 100 Important Automotive Terms

ABS (Anti-lock Braking System)
A safety system that prevents wheels from locking during braking.
Accelerator
Pedal that controls engine power by regulating fuel and air intake.
Air Filter
A device that removes dust and debris from air entering the engine.
Alternator
Converts mechanical energy into electrical energy to charge the battery.
Axle
Shaft that connects wheels and supports vehicle weight.
Battery
Stores electrical energy to start the engine and power electrical systems.
Bearing
Component that reduces friction between moving parts.
Brake Caliper
Part of disc brake system that presses brake pads on the rotor.
Brake Pad
Friction material that presses against brake rotor to slow vehicle.
Brake Rotor (Disc)
Rotating part pressed by brake pads to stop the wheel.
Camshaft
Shaft that opens and closes engine valves.
Carburetor
Device that mixes air and fuel for combustion (older engines).
Catalytic Converter
Emission control device converting harmful gases to less toxic.
Chassis
Frame that supports the vehicle body and components.
Clutch
Device that connects and disconnects the engine from transmission.
Combustion
Burning of fuel-air mixture inside engine cylinders.
Compression Stroke
Engine cycle phase where air-fuel mixture is compressed.
Crankshaft
Converts piston movement into rotational motion.
CV Joint (Constant Velocity Joint)
Allows smooth power transmission to wheels.
Cylinder
Chamber where combustion occurs.
Cylinder Head
Top part of cylinder enclosing valves and spark plugs.
Differential
Allows wheels to rotate at different speeds during turns.
Dipstick
Tool to check engine oil level.
Drive Shaft
Transmits power from transmission to differential/wheels.
Drum Brake
Brake system using brake shoes inside a drum.
EFI (Electronic Fuel Injection)
Computer-controlled fuel delivery system.
Engine Block
Main structure housing cylinders and pistons.
Exhaust Manifold
Collects exhaust gases from cylinders.
Fan Belt
Drives cooling fan and accessories from the engine.
Fuel Injector
Device that sprays fuel into combustion chamber.
Fuel Pump
Pumps fuel from tank to engine.
Fuel Tank
Stores vehicle fuel.
Fusible Link
Electrical safety device that melts to protect circuits.
Generator
Older term for alternator.
Headlight
Front light for night driving.
Ignition Coil
Converts battery voltage to high voltage for spark plugs.
Ignition System
Creates spark to ignite fuel-air mixture.
Injector
See Fuel Injector.
Intake Manifold
Distributes air to cylinders.
Lug Nut
Fastens wheel to axle.
Master Cylinder
Converts brake pedal force to hydraulic pressure.
Muffler
Reduces engine exhaust noise.
OBD-II (On-Board Diagnostics)
Standard vehicle self-diagnostic system.
Oil Filter
Removes contaminants from engine oil.
Oxygen Sensor
Measures oxygen in exhaust for fuel control.
Overdrive
Gear ratio for fuel-efficient cruising.
Piston
Moves inside cylinder to compress air-fuel mixture.
Power Steering
System that reduces steering effort.
Radiator
Cools engine coolant.
Rearview Mirror
Provides rear vision to driver.
Reciprocating Motion
Back and forth movement (e.g., piston).
Relay
Electrically operated switch.
Reservoir Tank
Stores excess fluid (coolant, brake).
Resistance
Electrical opposition to current.
Rotor
Part of ignition system producing spark.
Safety Glass
Laminated glass to protect occupants.
Sensor
Detects physical parameters (temperature, pressure).
Shock Absorber
Controls suspension movement.
Spark Plug
Produces spark to ignite fuel.
Starter Motor
Cranks engine to start.
Steering Column
Connects steering wheel to steering gear.
Suspension
System supporting vehicle and absorbing shocks.
Throttle
Controls engine power by regulating airflow.
Timing Belt/Chain
Synchronizes camshaft and crankshaft.
Torque
Rotational force produced by engine.
Transaxle
Combined transmission and differential.
Transmission
Transfers engine power to wheels with gear ratios.
Turbocharger
Forces extra air into engine for power.
Universal Joint
Flexible joint in drive shaft.
Valve
Controls intake and exhaust flow in engine.
Valve Spring
Returns valve to closed position.
Valve Timing
Synchronization of valves with piston movement.
Wiper Motor
Powers windshield wipers.
Wheel Alignment
Adjustment of wheel angles.
Wheel Bearing
Supports wheel rotation.
Windshield
Front glass protecting driver.
Wiring Harness
Bundle of electrical wires.
Yaw Sensor
Detects vehicle rotation for stability control.
Zinc Coating
Rust protection on metal parts.
Air Conditioning (A/C)
Cools vehicle interior.
ABS Module
Controls ABS system.
Backup Camera
Rear vision aid.
Brake Booster
Amplifies brake pedal force.
Camber
Wheel tilt angle.
Catalyst
Substance facilitating chemical reaction.
Cylinder Deactivation
Shutting down cylinders to save fuel.
Dead Reckoning
Navigation by estimating position.
Diff Lock
Locks differential to improve traction.
Drivetrain
All components transmitting power to wheels.
ECU (Engine Control Unit)
Computer managing engine functions.
Exhaust Pipe
Conveys exhaust gases away.
Fuel Economy
Efficiency of fuel consumption.
Glow Plug
Heats diesel engine combustion chamber.
Harmonic Balancer
Reduces engine vibrations.
Idle Speed
Engine speed at rest.
Knock Sensor
Detects engine knocking.
Lift Pump
Transfers fuel from tank to injection pump.
Load Sensing Valve
Adjusts brake pressure by load.
MAP Sensor
Measures intake pressure (Manifold Absolute Pressure).
Mild Hybrid
Vehicle with electric assist but no pure EV mode.

Short Questions & Answers: Automotive Systems & Functions

Short Questions & Answers: Automotive Systems & Functions

1. Safety and Workshop Practices

Q: Why is PPE important in the workshop?
A: PPE protects workers from injuries caused by chemicals, sharp objects, noise, and other hazards.

Q: What are the key safety rules in an automotive workshop?
A: Keep work area clean, use tools properly, wear PPE, follow emergency procedures, and respect equipment.

2. Engine System

Q: What is the difference between SI and CI engines?
A: SI (Spark Ignition) engines use spark plugs for ignition; CI (Compression Ignition) engines ignite fuel by compression heat.

Q: What are the four strokes in a 4-stroke engine?
A: Intake, Compression, Power (Combustion), and Exhaust.

Q: What is the role of the camshaft?
A: It controls the opening and closing of intake and exhaust valves.

3. Fuel System

Q: What is the function of a fuel injector?
A: To spray fuel into the combustion chamber in a precise amount and pattern.

Q: Why is a fuel filter important?
A: It removes impurities from fuel to protect engine components.

4. Cooling System

Q: What does the thermostat do in the cooling system?
A: Regulates coolant flow to maintain engine temperature.

Q: What is the purpose of the radiator?
A: To dissipate heat from the coolant to the outside air.

5. Lubrication System

Q: Why is engine oil important?
A: It lubricates moving parts, reduces friction, and cools the engine.

Q: What does an oil filter do?
A: Removes contaminants from engine oil.

6. Starting System

Q: What is the main function of the starter motor?
A: To crank the engine for starting.

Q: How does the ignition switch work?
A: It sends power to the starter and ignition system.

7. Ignition System

Q: What is the role of the ignition coil?
A: Converts battery voltage into high voltage for the spark plugs.

Q: What causes engine misfire?
A: Faulty spark plugs, ignition coil, or fuel delivery issues.

8. Air Induction System

Q: Why is an air filter important?
A: It prevents dust and debris from entering the engine.

Q: What is throttle body‚Äôs role?
A: Controls the amount of air entering the engine.

9. Exhaust System

Q: What is the purpose of a catalytic converter?
A: To reduce harmful emissions by converting gases to less toxic substances.

Q: Why is exhaust leak dangerous?
A: It can allow toxic gases into the passenger cabin and reduce engine efficiency.

10. Engine Tune-Up

Q: What is involved in a basic SI engine tune-up?
A: Checking ignition timing, replacing spark plugs, and adjusting carburetor or fuel injection.

Q: How does diesel injector testing help?
A: It ensures proper fuel spray and combustion.

11. Powertrain and Steering

Q: What is the role of the clutch?
A: Connects/disconnects the engine power to the transmission.

Q: What does power steering do?
A: Reduces driver effort to turn the steering wheel.

12. Suspension and Alignment

Q: What is camber angle?
A: The tilt of the wheel relative to vertical.

Q: Why is wheel alignment important?
A: Ensures proper tire wear and vehicle handling.

13. Braking System

Q: How does ABS improve braking?
A: Prevents wheel lock-up during emergency braking.

Q: What is brake bleeding?
A: Removing air from the brake hydraulic system.

14. Air Conditioning (A/C)

Q: What does the refrigerant do in A/C?
A: Absorbs heat from inside the vehicle and releases it outside.

Q: Why check blower motor?
A: To ensure proper airflow inside the cabin.

15. Electrical System

Q: What does the alternator do?
A: Charges the battery and powers electrical systems when engine runs.

Q: What is a fuse?
A: A safety device that breaks the circuit if current is too high.

16. EFI System

Q: What is EFI?
A: Electronic Fuel Injection system that controls fuel delivery.

Q: Why clean fuel injectors?
A: To maintain optimal spray pattern and engine performance.

17. Charging System

Q: What components are in charging system?
A: Battery, alternator, voltage regulator.

Q: What happens if alternator fails?
A: Battery will drain, electrical systems fail.

18. LPG/CNG Systems

Q: Why convert to LPG/CNG?
A: Lower fuel cost and emissions.

Q: What safety checks are necessary?
A: Leak tests, regulator function, and secure fittings.

19. Hybrid & EV Systems

Q: What is a hybrid vehicle?
A: Combines internal combustion engine with electric motor.

Q: What is CAN bus?
A: Communication network for vehicle electronics.

20. ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems)

Q: Name one ADAS feature.
A: Lane departure warning.

Q: Why is ADAS important?
A: Improves safety by assisting driver.
